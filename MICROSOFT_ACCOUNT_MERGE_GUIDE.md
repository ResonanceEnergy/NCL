## 📋 **Microsoft Account Merge Process**

### **Step 1: Prepare Your Accounts**
Before requesting a merge, ensure:
- All accounts have valid email access
- No active subscriptions that might conflict
- Important data is backed up
- You know the recovery options for each account

### **Step 2: Contact Microsoft Support**
Microsoft account merges require direct support intervention:

**Option A: Microsoft Account Support**
1. Go to: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/account-billing/contact-microsoft-account-support-4c4e1f1d-4c4e1f1d
2. Sign in with the account you want to **keep** as primary
3. Select "Account" → "Merging accounts"
4. Explain your situation and provide all account details

**Option B: Business Account Support (for onmicrosoft.com)**
1. Go to: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/topic/contact-support-for-business-products-admin-help-5b3b6b4b-8b4b-4b4b-8b4b-8b4b8b4b8b4b
2. Select your product (Microsoft 365, Azure, etc.)
3. Choose "Billing & Subscriptions" → "Account management"

### **Step 3: Provide Required Information**
When contacting support, have ready:
- All three email addresses
- Associated phone numbers (if any)
- Recovery email addresses
- Security information/proof of ownership
- Reason for merge (consolidating Super Agency operations)

### **Step 4: Choose Primary Account**
Decide which account will be your primary:
- **Recommendation**: `nate@gripandripp.onmicrosoft.com` (business account)
- This account can better serve your Super Agency operations
- Outlook accounts can be migrated into business accounts

## 🔄 **Alternative Approaches**

### **Option 1: Manual Data Migration**
If Microsoft cannot merge directly:
1. Export data from secondary accounts
2. Import to primary account
3. Update all service associations
4. Close secondary accounts

### **Option 2: Account Aliases**
Add secondary emails as aliases to your primary account:
1. Go to account.microsoft.com
2. Add aliases under "Your info" → "Manage how you sign in"
3. This allows access without full merge
