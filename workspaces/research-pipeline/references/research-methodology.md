# UNI Research Methodology

## Multi-Source Verification

All research findings must be verified across at least 2 independent sources before being classified as high-confidence.

### Source Hierarchy
1. **Primary sources**: Academic papers, official documents, direct data
2. **Verified secondary**: Established journalism, verified expert analysis
3. **Intelligence signals**: Social media, forums, prediction markets
4. **Speculative**: Unverified claims, single-source reports

### Confidence Scoring
- 0.9-1.0: Multiple primary sources confirm
- 0.7-0.9: Primary + secondary sources align
- 0.5-0.7: Secondary sources only, no contradictions
- 0.3-0.5: Single source or conflicting signals
- 0.0-0.3: Speculative, insufficient evidence

## Research Pipeline Stages

1. **Source Scan** (Stage 01): Cast wide net across all configured sources
2. **Extraction** (Stage 02): Pull relevant data points, quotes, metrics
3. **Analysis** (Stage 03): Cross-reference, identify patterns, score confidence
4. **Convergence** (Stage 04): Check for alignment with other active research and intelligence signals
5. **Archive** (Stage 05): Package findings with full source chain and store

## Tools
- Perplexity: Fact-checking and real-time web search
- Gemini: YouTube analysis and Google Trends
- Local models (Ollama): Fast preliminary analysis
- Claude: Deep analysis and synthesis (council chair)
