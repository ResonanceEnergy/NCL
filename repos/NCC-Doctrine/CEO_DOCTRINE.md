## 4. CRISIS MANAGEMENT AUTHORITY

### 4.1 Emergency Command Protocols

#### Crisis Declaration Authority
The CEO has unilateral authority to declare agency-wide crises:

**Crisis Levels:**
- **Level 1:** Operational disruption (CEO discretion)
- **Level 2:** Financial threat (CEO + CFO consultation)
- **Level 3:** Existential threat (Full executive team)
- **Level 4:** Mission collapse (CEO emergency authority)

#### EXECUTIVE_OVERRIDE Protocol

**Activation Conditions:**
- Immediate threat to agency survival
- Council 52 compromise or failure
- NCC critical system failure
- Executive decision paralysis

**Override Procedure:**
1. CEO declares "EXECUTIVE_OVERRIDE - [Reason]"
2. Automatic notification to executive team
3. NCC switches to manual command mode
4. Council 52 suspends autonomous operations
5. CEO assumes direct operational control
6. 24-hour post-override review mandatory

### 4.2 Crisis Response Framework

#### Phase 1: Crisis Assessment (0-30 minutes)
- CEO crisis command center activation
- Executive team emergency briefing
- NCC crisis intelligence gathering
- Initial impact assessment

#### Phase 2: Crisis Management (30 minutes - 24 hours)
- CEO command decisions
- Executive team action assignment
- Council 52 crisis intelligence processing
- Stakeholder communication protocols

#### Phase 3: Crisis Resolution (24+ hours)
- Recovery plan implementation
- Executive debrief and lessons learned
- NCC system restoration
- Stakeholder relationship management
