# FRACTAL FUTURE — Experiment 1: Fractal Calm

ID: FF-EXP-001
Track: Human Resonance Lab
Status: Design
Gate: FF-2 (Observation)
Evidence Target: E2 → E3

## Hypothesis
Breathing patterns that follow fractal timing (variable intervals with self-similar structure) produce greater stress reduction and higher heart rate variability (HRV) than fixed-interval breathing (e.g., box breathing at constant 4-4-4-4).

## Background
- Fractal variability in heart rate is a marker of health (Goldberger, 1996)
- Loss of fractal HRV correlates with disease and aging
- If fractal OUTPUT (HRV) indicates health, does fractal INPUT (breathing pattern) enhance it?
- Box breathing is effective but rigid — entropy-suppressing rather than entropy-channeling

## Protocol

### Phase 1 — Baseline (Week 1)
1. Measure resting HRV daily for 7 days (no breathing intervention)
2. Record subjective stress (1–10 scale) morning and evening
3. Establish personal baseline HRV metrics

### Phase 2 — Control: Box Breathing (Week 2)
1. 10 minutes daily box breathing (4-4-4-4 fixed cadence)
2. Measure HRV immediately before, during, and after
3. Record subjective stress before and after

### Phase 3 — Intervention: Fractal Breathing (Weeks 3–4)
1. 10 minutes daily fractal breathing pattern:
   - Base cycle: 4-count inhale, 4-count exhale
   - Variation: Every 3rd cycle, extend to 6-count inhale, 6-count exhale
   - Every 7th cycle, extend to 8-count inhale, 8-count exhale
   - Pattern: 4,4,4,6,4,4,4,4,6,4,4,4,4,6,8,4,4,4,6... (self-similar nesting)
2. Measure HRV immediately before, during, and after
3. Record subjective stress before and after

### Phase 4 — Analysis (Week 5)
1. Compare HRV metrics across baseline, box, and fractal phases
2. Compare subjective stress reduction
3. Statistical significance test (paired t-test or Wilcoxon)

## Variables
