# ENTROPY GATE — FF-6 (NCC Governance Standard)

## Purpose
Prevent hidden entropy accumulation and prevent systems that externalize entropy onto humans, users, or the environment.

---

## Gate FF-6: Entropy Gate (Pass/Fail)
Any new doctrine claim, system design, strategy, or agent workflow must answer:

### 1) Entropy Ingress
Where does entropy enter? (noise, drift, shocks, adversaries, time)

### 2) Accumulation Zones
Where does entropy pile up? (queues, complexity, coupling, debt, overload)

### 3) Dissipation Paths
Where does entropy exit safely? (pruning, resets, cooldowns, circuit breakers, throttles)

### 4) Failure Containment
What fails first? Does it fail locally? What is the blast radius?

### 5) Maintenance Load
What ongoing effort is required to keep coherence? Is it bounded or unbounded?

---

## Auto-Reject Conditions
- Entropy is ignored or hand-waved
- The design requires perfect/ideal conditions to function
- Failure cascades are plausible and uncontained
- Maintenance grows superlinearly with scale
- Disorder is offloaded onto humans (attention, stress, coercion)

## Pass Conditions (Preferred)
- Clear entropy map exists
- Entropy budget exists (thresholds defined)
- Entropy valves exist (safe release mechanisms designed in)
- Fail-local containment is explicit in the design

---

## Entropy Map Method (Universal)
For any system (human / AI / org / market / software):

1. **Ingress** — where disorder enters
2. **Accumulation** — where it piles up
3. **Dissipation** — how it safely exits
4. **Budgets** — thresholds before degradation
5. **Valves** — throttles / resets / pruning / circuit breakers
6. **Containment** — fail-local vs cascade

This becomes a required section in:
- NCC doctrine proposals
- NCL major features
- AAC strategies
- BRS programs
