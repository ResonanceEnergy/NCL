## 3) Definitions

### Fractal
A structure exhibiting:
- Self-similarity across scale
- Recursion and feedback
- Scale invariance (pattern integrity preserved under zoom)
- Complexity that remains coherent rather than random

### Sacred Geometry
Geometric relationships that:
- Recur across nature and human culture
- Encode stable proportions and efficient structures
- Are often perceived as harmonious by humans

**Note:** "Sacred" here means *deeply aligned with natural pattern law*, not belief-dependent.

### Fractal Intelligence
An intelligence that:
- Learns recursively via feedback loops
- Preserves coherence across scale
- Fails locally rather than catastrophically
- Optimizes long-horizon integrity over short-term extraction

### Entropy (Operational)
In this doctrine, entropy means:
- Loss of usable structure
- Increasing disorder/noise
- Drift away from intended function
- Rising maintenance cost to sustain coherence

## PRINCIPLE 0 — ENTROPY PRESSURE (ROOT)
> Every system exists under constant entropic pressure: decay, drift, noise, overload, and shock. Design must assume non-ideal conditions by default.

### Why Entropy Is the Root
- Entropy is the force that destroys structure
- Fractals are the structures that survive entropy
- Nature doesn't build fractals because they're pretty—it builds them because they are the most entropy-resistant structures known

### Fractals Solve Four Entropy Problems Simultaneously
1. **Energy dissipation without breakdown** — Trees dissipate wind via branching; rigid systems snap
2. **Transport with minimal loss** — Blood vessels, roots, neural networks maximize surface area while minimizing cost
3. **Local failure containment** — Lose a leaf ≠ lose the tree; lose neurons ≠ lose the mind
4. **Scale invariance under stress** — Growth doesn't require redesign; stress doesn't require central coordination

### Required Questions (for any design/claim)
1. Where does entropy enter?
2. Where does entropy accumulate?
3. How does entropy dissipate safely?
4. What fails first, and does it cascade?
5. What is the bounded maintenance load?

## 4) First Principles (v1.1)

### P1 — Scale Coherence
A system must remain interpretable and stable at micro/meso/macro levels. If it breaks at scale, it is not complete.

### P2 — Recursive Learning
Learning is iterative. Each loop increases resolution: observe → synthesize → test → refine. Learning loops are how systems export entropy as information.

### P3 — Local Autonomy, Global Harmony
Healthy systems allow local agency while maintaining global coherence (cells in a body, branches of a tree, nodes in a neural network). Local adaptation is entropy containment.

### P4 — Graceful Failure
Failure should be local and informative; cascading collapse is a design defect. Catastrophic failure is a design flaw, not an accident.

### P5 — Resonance Over Control
Control creates resistance; forcing systems increases entropy. Resonance aligns with existing dynamics, amplifying what works and damping what destabilizes.

### P6 — Regeneration Over Extraction
Systems must renew their inputs and outputs; growth that damages its source violates the doctrine. Extraction accelerates entropy; regeneration stabilizes it.

### P7 — Entropy Budgeting (NEW)
Systems must define an entropy budget (tolerable disorder rate) and enforce constraints that prevent runaway drift or collapse.

### P8 — Entropy Valves (NEW)
Systems must include safe pressure-release mechanisms (resets, throttles, cooldowns, pruning, circuit breakers) to prevent catastrophic failure.
