## Claim Statement

Systems that persist (trees, river networks, lungs, neural networks, market structures) do so because fractal organization maximizes surface area, distributes load, and enables graceful failure — all of which are entropy dissipation strategies.

1. **CC-001a**: Self-similar branching patterns appear wherever a system must dissipate entropy across scales (vascular systems, river deltas, lightning, neural dendrites).
2. **CC-001b**: Systems that lose fractal coherence accumulate entropy faster and become brittle (monoculture forests, rigid bureaucracies, over-fitted trading strategies).
3. **CC-001c**: Entropy budgeting and valve design (P7, P8) are the engineering equivalents of what nature does automatically through fractal adaptation.
