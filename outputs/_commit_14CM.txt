Wave 14CM: ticker-universe whitelist — XREF + trend tracker stop firing on common English words

NATRIX trial-run pushback: XREF is "just hot words and waste of time
and compute". Audit confirmed: 49 XREF ticker cards, 85% of which
were common English words misidentified as tickers:
  NOW REST THIS FREE OS TV PC NEED YOUR WILL ANY CAN POINT DM AMA
  III LIVE GOP DEV IA GREAT EA WR JUST MLB SORRY UI FC ITS IRS
  MVP UC DIRE TRADE DON HR NEVER THEY ONLY

Root cause: bare-uppercase regex `\b[A-Z]{2,5}\b` matches every
ALL-CAPS English word, and the 80-word stoplist couldn't catch
them all (English has thousands of 2-5 letter caps abbreviations).

Fix: universe whitelist — only emit ticker convergences for symbols
that appear in a curated known-good universe. The `$TICKER` notation
stays always-trusted (operators don't prefix English words with $).
Bare matches now must clear `is_valid_ticker()`.

NEW runtime/intelligence/ticker_universe.py (~150 LOC)
  Built-in seed universe (~400 unique symbols):
    - S&P 100 mega-caps (AAPL/MSFT/NVDA/etc.)
    - Major ETFs (SPY/QQQ/IWM + sector SPDRs + crypto ETFs +
      leveraged + commodities + thematic)
    - Popular retail/meme stocks (GME/PLTR/HOOD/RIOT/MSTR/etc.)
    - Top 50 crypto symbols
    - Macro tickers (WTI/BRENT/NIFTY/VIX/DXY/etc.)
  Optional operator extensions via data/tickers/universe.txt
  (one symbol per line, # comments). get_universe() merges both;
  reload_universe() invalidates the cache.
