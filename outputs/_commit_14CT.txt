Root causes in priority order:

1. **brief stocks tagged strategy="stock"** → _normalize_strategy
   returned "unknown" → governor capped at 5% NAV → "Strategy
   'unknown' heat would breach cap" reject. (18 historical
   rejects with this reason.)

2. **R_per_share never computed** → policy "no R_per_share"
   reject. (14 of 20 24h chains.) Brief gives entry + stop;
   R is trivially |entry - stop|.

3. **options ideas mix premium with underlying price** — brief
   chair emits "$110 put with $20 max_risk and $50 target",
   where entry is the OPTION premium and target/stop refer to
   underlying. Synthesizing a stock-style stop from max_risk
   produced trades with insane R:R (MSFT registered as
   entry=450/stop=350/target=40 → governor saw R=$100 ×
   qty=100 = $10K, breaching 25% total cap).

4. **null entry/stop/target** ideas (1 of 20) wasted a chain
   slot.

5. **stop_type=None** passed the gate but my 14CT-v1 default
   "absolute" wasn't in policy.VALID_STOP_TYPES (price/atr/
   volatility/time/thesis_break).

Fixes:

  runtime/portfolio/risk_governor.py _normalize_strategy
    - Map "stock"/"stocks"/"equity"/"brief"/"brief_stock" → "goat"
      (brief stocks are momentum-style — goat is the right bucket)
    - Map "future"/"futures"/"es"/"nq" → "manual" placeholder
      (no futures budget bucket yet)

  runtime/intelligence/brief_archiver.py _register_trade_ideas
    - Normalize entry/stop/target FIRST then compute
      R_per_share = round(abs(entry - stop), 4) if missing
    - SKIP type="options" ideas entirely (defer until brief
      gets proper strike/DTE/premium schema). The fake-stop
      synthesis from max_risk was producing nonsense.
    - SKIP ideas where any of entry/stop/target is None
    - Default stop_type to "price" (in VALID_STOP_TYPES)
